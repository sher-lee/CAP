CAP (Cytology Analysis Project) is a veterinary ear-cytology automation system designed for acquisition by IDEXX Laboratories in the $5–10M range. The system’s primary value proposition is high-quality slide imaging and client visualization: producing sharp, detailed microscopy images that veterinarians can show pet owners in the exam room to explain findings and justify treatment. AI-powered organism detection is a secondary efficiency add-on that automates counting and classification for the technician workflow.
The system combines three pillars in priority order: (1) robotic slide scanning and high-resolution imaging for client communication, (2) client-facing visualization including stitched whole-slide images, zoomable viewers, and auto-generated PDF reports, and (3) AI organism detection to reduce technician manual counting time. All architectural decisions, particularly camera selection and image processing pipeline design, prioritize image quality for the client-facing output over scan speed or AI optimization.
The system runs entirely on an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano, operates offline without internet dependency, and is designed for single-user, single-slide-at-a-time clinic workflows. The AI model (YOLOv11) is currently at 36.7% precision with 188 training images and is expected to improve through the integrated retraining pipeline. Importantly, the system delivers immediate value through imaging alone, independent of AI model maturity.
